Background

Missy is a nationally certified and state licensed Occupational Therapist, Driving Rehab Specialist and Driving Instructor.

With over 25 years experience working with diverse populations across the lifespan, most of her focus has been on supporting and empowering persons/families dealing with invisible differencesand helping those who are underserved by health system cracks. She has held pediatric practice emphasis since 2002 but began training to work with adult driving rehab needs after getting so many specialty-case adult referrals to her pediatric driving program.

Her OT/Driving Rehabilitation experience began in 2001 at North Kansas City Hospital. There she completed clinical evaluation and potential to drive assessments with adults due to age-related or disability factors including Stroke, Dementia, Alzheimer’s, Brain Injury, Parkinson’s/MS, and Orthopedic challenges.

Helping families and adults with “hidden disabilities” is her primary passion along with driving. Missy created and ran a specialty therapy and educational support services clinic called Extra Credit! LLC. A major success of her business was related to a Driving Readiness Program (called DRT). Much of her work was based on specialized training she received from Dr. Miriam Monahan, an expert in the field of driving rehabilitation and extensive educator in the field. Dr. Monahan granted permission for some of her own training materials to be used in the DRT and a supplemental helper packet. Local driving specialists at St. David’s Adaptive Driving program still use Missy’s Driver Helper Program as a treatment reference.

Goals and Specialization

Missy has trained Region XIII educators in their role with teen driving safety. She has written a safe driving blog for DriveItHome.org and was invited to speak at the National Transportation and Highway Safety Conference of 2017. She is an ADED member, AOTA trained/listed driving rehab provider, and Carfit-certified OT technician. While advancing hours for DRS certification and for additional knowledge and advanced scope of service, Missy has mentors across the country that she regularly collaborates with, including local area specialists.

Missy is AOTA educated in the highest level of Community Mobility and Driving with Adults (Badge I, II, III holder) and received one of the few Adolescent Driving badges ever issued from AOTA due to her own OT program development. Missy has completed advanced OT/DRS training by Adaptive Mobility Services, the professional training called Behind the Wheel with ADHD, and promotes safe driving products across the field while advancing towards her goals ofCDRS and SOTC.
